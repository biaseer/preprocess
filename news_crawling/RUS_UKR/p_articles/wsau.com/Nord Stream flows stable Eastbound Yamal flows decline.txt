Eastbound gas flows via the Yamal-Europe pipeline to Poland from Germany declined on Friday morning, data from pipeline operator Gascade showed.
Gazprom said its supply of gas to Europe through Ukraine via the Sudzha entry point was expected at 41.9 mcm on Friday, down slightly from 42.2 mcm a day earlier.
While flows remained at pre-maintenance level of 40 capacity, concerns about full halt of Russian flows via the pipeline remain due to political uncertainty.
On the Nord Stream AG website, physical flows were at 29,191,527 kWhh for 0700-0800 CET 0500-0600 GMT, compared with levels around 29,000,000 kWhh the previous day.
LONDON Reuters      Physical flows of Russian gas through Nord Stream 1 pipeline to Germany remained stable on Friday, while eastbound gas flows via the Yamal-Europe pipeline to Poland from Germany declined, operators data showed.
The pipeline, which accounts for more than a third of Russian gas exports to the European Union, resumed on Thursday after a 10-day outage due to maintenance.
Exit flows at the Mallnow metering point on the German border stood at 2,652,767 kWhh versus levels around 3,911,000 kWhh the previous day.
Nominations for Russian gas flows into Slovakia from Ukraine via the Velke Kapusany border point remained steady on Friday and stood at 36.9 million cubic metres mcm per day, unchanged from the previous day, data from the Ukrainian transmission system operator showed.
