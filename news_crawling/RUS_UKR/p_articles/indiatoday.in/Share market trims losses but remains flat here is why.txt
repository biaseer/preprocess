Both indexes have clocked losses of more than 1 per cent in February as simmering tensions over Ukraine and the possibility of monetary tightening by the US Federal Reserve weighed on investor sentiment.
All major Nifty sub-indexes were trading in the red, with Nifty's realty .NIFTYREAL and metal NIFTYMET sectors slipping 2.6 per cent and 1.8 per cent, respectively.
If Monday's losses hold, both the Nifty and Sensex will record their fourth day of declines in a row.
Offering a measure of relief, however, U.S. President Joe Biden and Russia's Vladimir Putin agreed in principle to hold a summit on Ukraine. The news boosted Wall Street futures and helped wider Asian shares pare sharp early losses.
The benchmark Sensex was flat at 57,981.65 and the Nifty was marginally up at 17,308.60.
Shares of InterGlobe Aviation INGL.NS fell as much as 3.8 per cent after co-founder Rakesh Gangwal resigned from the board of the IndiGo airline operator and said he would cut his stake slowly over the next five years.
"Russia-Ukraine still remains the backdrop, it has dulled risk appetite and there aren't any cues to help domestic markets higher this morning," said Anand James, chief market strategist at Geojit Financial Services, according to a Reuters report.
"There is a little bit of hesitance going into March, traders at the moment are discouraged from taking heavy bets, and with the Fed's rate decision due in March, its the moment of truth for the markets and one has to wait and see how things will transpire," Anand James said.
In India, 47 of the 50 stocks on the Nifty 50 index slipped. Coal India COAL.NS led losses with a 4 per cent drop, the Reuters report says.
In early morning trade, the NSE Nifty 50 was down 0.85 per cent at 17,131.65, while the SP BSE Sensex fell 0.74 per cent to 57,408.44.
By India Today Web Desk: The share market on Monday remained volatile, dragged by losses across the board as the Russia-Ukraine standoff continued to dampen risk sentiment.
