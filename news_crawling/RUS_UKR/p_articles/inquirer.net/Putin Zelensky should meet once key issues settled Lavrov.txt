Russia sent tens of thousands of troops into Ukraine on Feb. 24 in what it called a special operation to degrade its southern neighbors military capabilities and root out people it called dangerous nationalists.
Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov attends a news conference following talks with President of the International Committee of the Red Cross ICRC Peter Maurer in Moscow, Russia March 24, 2022. Kirill KudryavtsevPool via REUTERSFile Photo
Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov said on Monday that a meeting between President Vladimir Putin and his Ukrainian counterpart Volodymyr Zelensky should happen once the two sides are closer to agreeing on key issues.
Ukrainian forces have mounted stiff resistance and the West has imposed sweeping sanctions on Russia in an effort to force it to withdraw its forces.FEATURED STORIES
Speaking to Serbian media outlets, Lavrov added that any meeting between Putin and Zelenskiy to exchange views on the conflict right now would be counter-productive.
