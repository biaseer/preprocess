Russian President Vladimir Putin has said Russias special operation in Ukraine was needed to protect people who have been subjected to bullying and genocide. By this meant those whose first or only language is Russian.
Russias talks with a hostile Ukraine have not been easy, but the main thing is that they are continuing, RIA news agency quoted Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov as saying on Saturday.
Peskov said Moscow had launched the invasion to save two eastern regions seized by Russian-backed separatists in 2014 and said he trusted that the Russian language would be restored to its rightful place in the country.
