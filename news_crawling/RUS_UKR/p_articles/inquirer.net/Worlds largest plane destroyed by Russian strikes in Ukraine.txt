Worlds largest plane destroyed by Russian strikes in Ukraine

KYIV  The largest plane in the world  Ukraines Antonov-225 cargo plane  was destroyed by Russian strikes outside Kyiv on the fourth day of Moscows invasion, Ukraines state-owned Ukroboronprom group said Sunday.
Russian invaders destroyed the flagship of the Ukrainian aviation, the AN-225 at the Antonov airport in Gostomel near Kyiv, the group said in a statement.
Initially built as part of the Soviet aeronautical program, the An-225 made its first flight in 1988.

The aircraft was unique to the world, at 84 meters long 276 feet it could transport up to 250 tonnes 551,000 pounds of cargo at a speed of up to 850 kilometers per hour 528 mph.
It has been operated by Ukraines Antonov Airlines for cargo flights and was in high demand during the start of the Covid-19 pandemic.
This was the worlds largest aircraft, AN-225 Mriya' Ukraine Foreign Minister Dmytro Kuleba tweeted Sunday.
After years of not flying after the fall of the Soviet Union, the only existing copy made a test flight in 2001 in Gostomel, about 20 kilometres from Kyiv.
Weapons manufacturer Ukroboronprom estimated that restoring the Mriya would cost over $3 billion 2.7 billion euros and could take over five years.
