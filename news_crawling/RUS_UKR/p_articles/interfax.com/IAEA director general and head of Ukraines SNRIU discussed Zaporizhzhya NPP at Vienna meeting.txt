Following the visit of IAEA inspectors and technicians to the Chernobyl NPP, data from all unattended monitoring systems installed at the site has now been fully recovered, it said.
Grossi visited the Chernobyl NPP on April 26, leading the IAEA mission to the site and delivering a batch of nuclear safety equipment.
"We took stock of progress in our nuclear safety and security assistance to Ukraine and spoke on the Zaporizhzhya NPP, where the IAEA is ready to fulfill its indispensable role," Grossi said on Twitter on Thursday.
KYIV. May 6 Interfax-Ukraine - Director General of the International Atomic Energy Agency IAEA Rafael Mariano Grossi and head of the State Nuclear Regulatory Inspectorate of Ukraine SNRIU Oleh Korikov discussed the situation around the Zaporizhzhya Nuclear Power Plant NPP, where the Russian Armed Forces are currently present, at a meeting at the IAEA Headquarters in Vienna.
SNRIU, in turn, said on social media that Korikov led Ukraine's delegation to the Fourth Extraordinary Meeting of the Contracting Parties to the Joint Convention on the Safety of Spent Fuel Management and on the Safety of Radioactive Waste Management.
In its daily update on Ukraine, the IAEA said that as a result of the deployment of new transmission channels based on satellite technologies, the remote transfer of the safeguards data to the IAEA's Vienna headquarters has been fully restored with the exception of one facility "for which technical work is still required".
