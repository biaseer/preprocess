Oil prices fell by nearly $4 a barrel on Monday after disappointing Chinese economic data renewed concerns of a global recession that would be expected to reduce fuel demand.
A damaged oil pipeline component that disrupted output at several offshore US Gulf of Mexico platforms was repaired late Friday, prompting oil producers to reactivate some of the halted production, a Louisiana official said last week. Supply disruptions at several offshore oil platforms within the Gulf coast region that added to last weeks price strength appear to have stabilised for now with output resuming, said Ritterbusch and Associates LLC in Galena, Illinois President Jim Ritterbusch.
