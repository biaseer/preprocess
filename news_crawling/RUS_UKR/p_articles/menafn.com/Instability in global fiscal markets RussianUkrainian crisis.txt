MENAFN The US Federal Reserve stated in a study released on Friday that geopolitical conflicts between Russia and Ukraine are causing instability in international fiscal and commodity markets.
Demand for oil surged as the international economy recovered from the crisis caused by Covid-19, according to the Fed, and oil costs proceeded to rise in the last six months of 2021 and into this current year.
The Fed stated in its fiscal policy statement to Congress that, "geopolitical tensions with Russia have also contributed to higher energy prices, including oil and natural gas."
