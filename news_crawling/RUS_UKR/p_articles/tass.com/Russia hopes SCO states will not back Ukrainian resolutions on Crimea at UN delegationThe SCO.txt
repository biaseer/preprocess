According to the sources, it was stated that the SCO member states act as a united front in the UN in support of the resolutions related to the 75th anniversary of the end of World War II, the fight against the glorification of Nazism, the non-deployment of weapons in space, the strengthening of the system of agreements in the field of disarmament and non-proliferation, as well as issues of international information security.
"Russia counts on the same solidarity in opposing counterproductive and politicized resolutions, openly aimed at one or another SCO member, against their interests. In particular, with regard to Russia, we mean resolutions that speculate on the subject of human rights in Crimea and on the situation in Abkhazia and South Ossetia after their declaration of independence in the face of aggression and as a defense against the aggressive actions of Tbilisi," the source stressed.
The SCO is a regional organization bringing together Russia, India, China, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Pakistan, Tajikistan and Uzbekistan. That said, Mongolia, Iran, Afghanistan and Belarus are observers. Armenia, Azerbaijan, Cambodia, Nepal, Turkey and Sri Lanka have the status of dialogue partners.
This is according to a statement, the Russian delegation made at a meeting of the Council of Foreign Ministers of the SCO member states in Dushanbe, a source with the delegation told TASS.
DUSHANBE, July 14. TASS. Russia expects that the member states of the Shanghai Cooperation Organization SCO will not support Ukraine's resolutions on Crimea and Georgias statements on Abkhazia and South Ossetia at the UN.
